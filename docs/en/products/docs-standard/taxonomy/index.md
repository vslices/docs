# Taxonomy

VSlices Docs Standard organizes documents by the kind of knowledge they preserve.

The goal of this taxonomy is to help teams choose the right document based on what they need to protect from being lost.

This taxonomy does not define a workflow. It does not say which document must be created first. It only explains what each document is best suited to preserve.

## Core idea

Different documents preserve different kinds of knowledge.

Any team should do a shift when using VSlices Docs Standard:

> Which document does the process require? ->  What kind of knowledge are we trying to preserve?

Because, if the team needs to preserve

- language, it may need a Domain Vocabulary.
- where work happens, it may need a Context Document.
- how work is performed, it may need a Process Document.
- behavior means, it may need a Use Case Document.
- what must be possible, it may need a Capability Document.
- why a direction was chosen, it may need a Decision Record.
- what was learned, it may need a Validation Note.
 
But also if the knowledge is still early, uncertain, local, or temporary, it may only need a Support Note.

## Taxonomy groups

The documents can also be understood as groups.

* **Language-oriented documents**: preserve terms and meanings that support shared understanding.
* **Context-oriented documents**: preserve scenarios and work lines so later decisions do not become isolated.
* **Operation-oriented documents**: preserve work processes and workflows so implementation reflects how work is performed.
* **Behavior-oriented documents**: preserve use cases so actions, consequences, validations, and errors remain explicit.
* **Capability-oriented documents**: preserve stable abilities before they become implementation structure.
* **Decision-oriented documents**: preserve reasoning, tradeoffs, and accepted consequences.
* **Learning-oriented documents**: preserve validation, feedback, and evidence gathered after review or implementation.
* **Support documents**: preserve early or uncertain knowledge before it earns more structure.

## How to choose a document

Use the smallest document that preserves the knowledge future work depends on.

* **Use Domain Vocabulary** when language may affect understanding, behavior, naming, or boundaries.
* **Use Context Document** when the team needs to preserve the scenario, work lines, actors, or surrounding situation.
* **Use Process Document** when responsibilities, coordination, workflows, handoffs, or operational rules matter.
* **Use Use Case Document** when behavior needs explicit meaning, consequences, validations, or expected errors.
* **Use Capability Document** when several behaviors or processes depend on a stable ability.
* **Use Decision Record** when a choice has meaningful tradeoffs, consequences, or future impact.
* **Use Validation Note** when evidence changes or confirms knowledge that future work depends on.
* **Use Support Note** when the knowledge is useful but still early, uncertain, local, or temporary.

## Taxonomy principle

The taxonomy follows one principle:

> Choose documents by the knowledge they preserve, not by the ceremony they suggest.

A document is useful when it protects continuity. If the knowledge can be preserved with a smaller artifact, use the smaller artifact.
