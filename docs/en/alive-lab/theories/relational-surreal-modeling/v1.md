# Relational Surreal Modeling

## Status

Exploratory

## Motivation

The construction of surreal numbers suggests that larger systems can emerge from relational definitions.

Surreal numbers do not replace real numbers. Instead, they contain them.

The larger structure preserves the smaller one while extending its expressive capabilities. This observation may have implications for software modeling.

## Core Idea

Traditional engineering descriptions often focus on objects. Examples:

* User
* Order
* Invoice
* Product

A relational approach focuses on the relationships between concepts. Examples:

Customer
→ places
→ Order

Order
→ generates
→ Invoice

Invoice
→ settles
→ Debt

The hypothesis is not that object-based modeling is incorrect. Is that relational modeling may represent a more general space which contains object-oriented descriptions as a subset.
