# Field Notes

Field Notes is a collection of lessons, observations, mistakes, realizations and patterns extracted from real software projects and professional experience.

Unlike the rest of the VSlices suite, Field Notes does not focus on:

- architecture
- implementation
- design
- documentation
- process

Instead, it focuses on the realities that emerge when people, teams, organizations and software interact over time.

## Why Field Notes Exists

Software is built by people.

- People make decisions.
- People make mistakes.
- People carry assumptions, ambitions, fears, strengths and limitations into every project they touch.

Many of the most expensive failures observed throughout software development are not caused by technology. They emerge from:

- misalignment
- unrealistic expectations
- poor communication
- invisible assumptions
- neglected mentorship
- organizational friction
- personal battles that nobody talks about until they become visible

Field Notes exists to preserve the lessons hidden inside those experiences.

## What Field Notes Is Not

Field Notes is not:

- psychology
- therapy
- coaching
- management consulting
- organizational doctrine
- universal truth

The notes represent lessons learned from specific situations. They should be treated as engineering reflections rather than prescriptions.

## Relationship With VSlices

The rest of the VSlices suite focuses on preserving continuity between:

- discovery
- documentation
- architecture
- implementation
- evolution

Field Notes extends that philosophy by preserving continuity of experience.

Its goal is to ensure that valuable lessons learned through real projects are not lost over time.

## A Note About Truth

Every Field Note begins with a realization and most realizations begin with an incorrect assumption.

For this reason, Field Notes should not be read as declarations of truth, but as corrections to a previous misunderstanding.

Many of the lessons documented here can be summarized as:

> I believed the problem was one thing.
>
> Over time, I discovered it was something else.

Those discoveries are the reason this collection exists.
