# All The Power In Those Hands...

> *Get a unsolicited hand amputation*

_When one expert becomes team infrastructure_

## Definition

> A pattern where a highly capable team member gradually becomes the default solution for an increasing number of problems.
>
> Over time, knowledge, ownership and decision-making become concentrated around that individual.
>
> The team becomes more efficient in the short term, but increasingly dependent in the long term.
>
> What initially appears as capability eventually becomes fragility.

## Context

This pattern commonly emerges when a team contains one or more individuals capable of solving problems significantly faster than others.

The behavior is usually reinforced by positive outcomes:

- Problems are resolved quickly.
- Delivery accelerates.
- Blockers disappear.
- Stakeholders become satisfied.
- The team gains confidence.

At first, everyone benefits.

## Observed Behavior

Several recurring patterns emerged:

- Difficult tasks were consistently assigned to the same person.
- Team members increasingly relied on that person for decisions.
- Knowledge transfer became less important than immediate delivery.
- Questions were redirected toward the same individual.
- The team optimized for execution rather than capability growth.
- Critical context became concentrated.
- Teaching became optional.
- Solving became expected.

The individual became more capable.

The team became less capable without them.

## Common Signals

Possible indicators that this pattern is occurring:

* "Let's ask them."
* "They know how this works."
* "It will be faster if they do it."
* One person reviews most critical decisions.
* One person becomes involved in most important initiatives.
* The same individual repeatedly unblocks the team.
* Knowledge is difficult to locate without speaking to a specific person.
* Progress slows noticeably when that person is unavailable.

## Underlying Tensions

### Delivery vs Capability Development

> Solving a problem creates output, teaching someone to solve it creates capacity.

### Helpfulness vs Dependency

> Every problem solved for someone is a problem they may never learn to solve themselves.

### Expertise vs Scalability

> Individual capability does not automatically scale to team capability.

### Ownership vs Concentration

> Ownership concentrated in one place eventually becomes risk.

## Consequences

### Short-Term

- Faster delivery.
- Improved problem resolution.
- Reduced blockers.
- Increased confidence in execution.

### Long-Term

- Knowledge silos.
- Reduced team autonomy.
- Increased bus factor.
- Senior engineer overload.
- Dependency on specific individuals.
- Slower team growth.
- Fragile delivery processes.

Ironically, the more capable the individual becomes, the more difficult it becomes for the team to operate without them.

## Reflection Questions

- Who would perform this task if this person was unavailable for a month?
- How much critical knowledge exists only in one person's head?
- Are we solving problems or multiplying capability?
- Does the team become stronger after each challenge?
- Are difficult tasks being delegated or centralized?
- Is this person creating independence or creating dependency?
- What happens when this person takes a vacation?
- What happens when this person leaves?

---

## Final Reflection

At first, it feels good.

- People trust you.
- Problems disappear when you touch them.
- The team moves faster because you are there.

The danger is that capability often feels like success long before dependency feels like risk.

A team that depends on a single pair of hands may appear efficient. Until those hands are no longer available.

> The reward for solving every problem is eventually becoming one.
