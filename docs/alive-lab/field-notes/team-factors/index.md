# Team Factors

Team Factors explores patterns that emerge between people working together toward a shared objective.

> Unlike Human Factors, the focus is not the individual.
>
> Unlike Organization Factors, the focus is not the organization.
> 
> The focus is the team itself.

Many software failures are not caused by technology, not even caused by organizational decisions.

Instead, they emerge from the interactions between people:

- how knowledge is shared
- how responsibilities evolve
- how trust develops
- how decisions are made
- how capability grows
- and how dependencies form

Teams are complex systems. Even when every individual acts with good intentions, the resulting dynamics can produce unexpected outcomes.

Team Factors exists to document those patterns when they appear in real projects.
